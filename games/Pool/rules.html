Pool Rules

Introduction

Pool is a popular cue sport played on a rectangular table covered with a cloth, where players use a cue stick to strike balls into pockets. The game is known for its strategic play and requires skill and precision.

The objective of Pool is to score points by pocketing balls in a specific order, depending on the variation of the game being played.

Game Setup

The Table

A standard Pool table is 7 to 9 feet long and has six pockets. The game is played with 16 balls: one cue ball and 15 object balls.

Object of the Game

Players aim to pocket their designated balls (stripes or solids) and then the 8-ball to win the game.

Scoring

Points are scored by successfully pocketing balls. The player who pockets the 8-ball after all their designated balls wins the game.

The Break

The game begins with a break shot, where the cue ball is struck from behind the head string to hit the racked balls. A legal break requires at least four object balls to hit a cushion or pocket an object ball.

Gameplay

Turns

Players take turns to shoot. A player continues their turn as long as they legally pocket a ball.

Fouls

Fouls occur when a player fails to hit their designated balls first, fails to hit any ball, or scratches the cue ball. Fouls result in the opponent getting ball-in-hand.

Winning the Game

The game is won by the first player to legally pocket the 8-ball after all their designated balls have been cleared from the table.
