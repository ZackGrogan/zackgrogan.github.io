Blackjack Rules

Introduction

Equally well known as Twenty-One, Blackjack is one of the most popular casino card games. The rules are simple, the play is thrilling, and there is opportunity for high strategy. For the expert player who mathematically plays a perfect game and is able to count cards, the odds are sometimes in that player's favor to win.

While the popularity of Blackjack dates from World War I, its roots go back to the 1760s in France, where it is called Vingt-et-Un (French for 21). Today, Blackjack is the one card game that can be found in every American casino.

Game Setup

The Pack

The standard 52-card pack is used, but in most casinos several decks of cards are shuffled together. The six-deck game (312 cards) is the most popular. A blank plastic card is placed toward the bottom to indicate when to reshuffle. When four or more decks are used, they are dealt from a shoe (a box that allows the dealer to remove cards one at a time).

Object of the Game

Each participant attempts to beat the dealer by getting a count as close to 21 as possible, without going over 21.

Betting

Before the deal begins, each player places a bet in the designated area. Minimum and maximum limits are established on the betting, and the general limits are from $2 to $500.

The Deal

When all players have placed their bets, the dealer gives one card face up to each player in rotation clockwise, and then one card face up to themselves. Another round of cards is then dealt face up to each player, but the dealer takes the second card face down.

Thus, each player except the dealer receives two cards face up, and the dealer receives one card face up and one card face down.

Naturals (Blackjack)

If a player's first two cards are an ace and a "ten-card" (a picture card or 10), giving a count of 21 in two cards, this is a natural or "blackjack."

  • If a player has a natural and the dealer does not, the dealer pays that player one and a half times their bet
  • If the dealer has a natural, they collect the bets of all players who do not have naturals
  • If both have naturals, it's a stand-off (tie) and the player takes back their chips

The Play

Player's Turn

Players must decide whether to "stand" (not ask for another card) or "hit" (ask for another card). A player may continue taking cards until they either "stand" or "bust" (go over 21).

Soft Hands

An ace with any other card is known as a "soft hand," as the ace can count as 1 or 11. For example, with a "soft 17" (ace and 6), the total can be 7 or 17.

Dealer's Play

When the dealer has served every player, their face-down card is turned up. The dealer must:

  • Stand on 17 or more
  • Hit on 16 or under
  • Count ace as 11 if it makes 17 or more (but not over 21)

Special Plays

Splitting Pairs

When dealt two cards of the same denomination, players may split them into two separate hands by placing an equal bet on each hand.

  • Split aces receive one card each and cannot draw again
  • Ten-card split with ace pays even money (not 1.5x)

Doubling Down

Players may double their bet when their first two cards total 9, 10, or 11. They receive exactly one more card face down, which isn't revealed until bets are settled.

Insurance

When the dealer's face-up card is an ace, players may bet up to half their original bet that the dealer has blackjack. This side bet pays 2:1 if the dealer has blackjack.

Basic Strategy

Based on Dealer's Upcard

  • Dealer shows 7-Ace: Draw until 17 or higher
  • Dealer shows 4-6: Stand on 12 or higher
  • Dealer shows 2-3: Stand on 13 or higher

Doubling Down Strategy

  • Always double on 11
  • Double on 10 unless dealer shows 10 or Ace
  • Double on 9 if dealer shows 2-6

Splitting Strategy

  • Always split Aces and 8s
  • Never split 10s or 5s
  • Don't split 4s (8 is good to draw to)
  • Split 2s, 3s, or 7s unless dealer shows 8-Ace
  • Split 6s only if dealer shows 2-6
